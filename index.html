Life Journey

Before I came to the U.S., I had got my B.S. and M.S. from the Computer Science Department at University of Science and Technology of China. In Fall 2004, I joined the Computer Science and Engineering Department at Arizona State University as a graduate student. This is the beginning of my journey towards a Ph.D. My adviser is Dr. K.Selcuk Candan. 

After graduating, I joined Teradata Corp., the world's largest company solely focused on creating enterprise agility through database software, enterprise data warehousing, data warehouse appliances, and analytics. Teradata has one of the best Parallel Database Management Systems (PDBMSs) in the world. My current work is on the integration of the Parallel DBMS with the Distributed File System (e.g., Hadoop DFS). 

Now I am working for Turn, a startup company of the digital advertising industry. Particularly, my work is to exploit the distributed computing technology (such as Hadoop MapReduce) for the construction of data platform to store and manage large scale data.

Research Interests

My primary research interest is in the field of data integration. Particularly, the existence of value and structural conflicts in the process of data integration prevents an effective exploration over integrated data. One goal of my work is to develop efficient algorithms to capture these conflicts and provide effective schemes for the conflict resolution.

Furthermore, other areas that I am interested in include but not limit to:

  • efficient twig-query processing over graph
  • topic segmentation of text streams
  • real-time XML stream filtering

Dream Tracking

Climbing is a difficult thing for people who get used to walking in the flat. It's like a long distance running. The hardest time is when you are in the middle of the mountain. Everything seems beyond what you can put up with. The breath is out of order, the legs weigh like stones, and the track under your feet extends out of sight. In a moment, nothing can catch you so really but the thought of giving up. How I want to take a break, or stop ahead! But the sun doesn't wait for anyone. It rises up as it does. I must make a choice between giving up and going on, or will miss the aim of the very climbing: the sun rise. In fact, when I set off, the choice has been made: I must continue, continue, and continue. In my heart, one thing is always there, which leads me forward, upward. It's the one that is hidden at the bottom of the Pandora's box. Hope...
